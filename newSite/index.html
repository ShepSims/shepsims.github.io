The crowd at SheepFest San Diego at one point numbered over 300,000 partygoers

The crowd at SheepFest San Diego at one point numbered over 300,000 partygoers, with the expansion of the venue allowing the festival to climb to extraordinary new heights. The festival on Nov 27th brought in hundreds of world class DJs and sprawled from the edges of Balboa Park through downtown, expanding towards OB and PB as the night drew on. The festival was supposed to go on for 7 hours, but as many people showed up as it got later, the DJs didnt stop and the party raged on for multiple days. As the population amassed, the large numbers allowed for many more DJs to set up at smaller stages, and an even larger crowd continued to grow as the city came alive.

SheepFest San Diego returns for its second year on Nov 27th, bigger, better and way more DJs

SheepFest, the massive San Diego festival, returned for its second year on Nov 27th, this time with an even bigger and better venue, more world class DJs, and an expected crowd of over 300,000 people. The festival, which spans the entire city, is one of the largest in the world and draws in partygoers from all over to experience the non-stop action.

San Diego's SheepFest consumes city

The festival started at Balboa Park and slowly grew to consume the whole city. The organizers drew inspiration from the European party scene and the underground San Diego rave scene, but the event was designed to appeal to a wider audience. The main attraction was the DJs set up on four yachts floating in the ocean off shore, each with its own theme and music genre. The parties on the yachts were open to anyone who wanted to attend, but the organizers also set up a lot of smaller events around San Diego, including art installations, parties in unique locations as well as pool parties. All venues were within walking distance of each other so attendees could wander around and find something fun or interesting to do at any time. There were also food vendors lining the streets offering samples of local cuisine. The festival took over the city, with parties and events happening all over. There were pool parties, yacht parties, and even an art installations.
